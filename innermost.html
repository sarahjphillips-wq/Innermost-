Innermost
· Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

What Innermost is

Innermost is a self-knowledge reflection app. It's built to hold small, real evidence of who you are, kept privately, for you.

What we collect

The entries you write or speak, an optional mood, optional tags, and a name if you choose to give one. If you choose a photo, that's stored too. We do not collect your location, contacts, browsing history, or anything from other apps.

Where it's stored

Everything is stored locally, in your browser's storage on your own device. It is not uploaded to a server or database we control. If you clear your browser data, reset the app, or switch devices, your entries do not transfer on their own — there is no automatic cloud backup. You can export your entries to a file yourself and restore them on another device.

When we send data elsewhere

Three features send text to Anthropic (the company behind Claude, the AI Innermost uses):

• Ask Innermost — your question and relevant saved entries are sent to generate an answer.

• Safety check — what you write is briefly checked for language suggesting risk of self-harm, so we can show you support resources if needed. This check does not store your text afterward, and if it flags something, that specific entry is not saved.

• Pattern tagging — each entry you save is briefly checked to see whether it shows a trait like courage or connection, so Ask Innermost can find it later. This happens automatically after saving, in the background. The tag itself (or no tag, if none applies) is stored with the entry on your device; the check does not keep a copy of your text afterward.

Outside of these three features, nothing you write is sent anywhere. We don't use analytics or ad tracking, and we don't sell or share your information with any third party for marketing.

If your entry suggests you may be at risk

If what you write suggests you may be thinking about suicide or self-harm, Innermost shows you crisis support resources (in New Zealand: call or text 1737, free, 24/7; emergency: 111). That entry is not saved to your record. This check is automated and is not a substitute for professional care.

Your rights

You can edit or delete any entry at any time from Your Record. You can also export everything to a file from Settings, and restore it later. You can clear all your data at any time from Settings. Because everything is stored on your own device, you are always in direct control of it — we do not hold a separate copy we could act on independently.

Children

Innermost is not directed at children and is not knowingly used to collect information from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in a meaningful way, we'll update the date at the top of this page.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent through the Report a Problem option in Settings.

· Terms of Use

Last updated: July 2026

1. What Innermost is

Innermost is a self-knowledge reflection app. You save short entries about your day, and later can ask Innermost a question, which it answers using only what you've saved. It is not a journal, a companion app, or a therapy tool.

2. What Innermost is not

Innermost is not a medical device, a therapy service, a crisis service, or a substitute for professional mental health care. It is not monitored by a clinician. Nothing Innermost shows you — including any automated safety message — is medical advice or a diagnosis.

3. Age requirement

Innermost is intended for use by adults. If you are under 18, please use Innermost only with the involvement of a parent or guardian.

4. Your account and your data

Innermost does not require an account. Your entries are stored locally on your own device. You are responsible for keeping your device secure. If you lose your device, reset the app, or clear your browser data, your entries may be permanently lost — Innermost does not currently back up your data elsewhere. You can export a copy of your entries to a file at any time from Settings, and restore from that file later. Where you keep that file, and who can see it, is then up to you.

5. The safety check feature

Innermost automatically checks what you write for language that may suggest risk of suicide or self-harm, and shows support resources if it finds a match. This is an automated, imperfect check — it will miss some things and may occasionally flag things it shouldn't. It is not a substitute for professional help. If you are in crisis, please contact emergency services or a crisis line directly.

6. Ask Innermost

When you use Ask Innermost, your question and relevant saved entries are sent to Anthropic's Claude AI to generate a response. Responses are generated automatically and may be incomplete, miss relevant entries, or occasionally be wrong. Ask Innermost does not give advice, medical guidance, or professional opinions — it reflects back your own words.

7. Pattern tagging

Each entry you save is automatically and briefly checked to see whether it shows a small number of specific traits, so that Ask Innermost can find it later when it's relevant. This happens in the background after saving and does not change or delay the save itself. This check may occasionally miss a trait an entry does show, or not apply one at all — most entries won't show any of these traits, and that's expected.

8. Acceptable use

Please don't use Innermost to store other people's private information without their knowledge, to harass or harm anyone, or for any unlawful purpose.

9. No warranty

Innermost is provided "as is," without warranty of any kind. We do not guarantee it will be error-free, uninterrupted, or fit for any particular purpose.

10. Limitation of liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, Innermost and its creator are not liable for any loss, damage, or harm arising from your use of the app, including loss of data or reliance on any automated response.

11. Changes to these terms

If these terms change in a meaningful way, we'll update the date at the top of this page.

12. Contact

Questions about these terms can be sent through the Report a Problem option in Settings.
